Welcome to the Research Homepage of Tanbir Ahmed, Ph. D.

About

Hello! Thank you for visiting my research homepage. I am an experimental mathematician with specialization in Extremal combinatorics and Combinatorial Optimization. I received my M. Sc. in Computer Science degree (2009) under the supervision of Prof. Vašek Chvátal in the area of combinatorial optimization and my Ph. D. in Computer Science degree (2013) under the supervision of Prof. Clement Lam in the area of extremal combinatorics, both from Concordia University. During 2013-2015, I continued as a research associate with Prof. Lam. I was a post-doc at Le Laboratoire d’Algèbre, de Combinatoire et d’Informatique Mathématique (LACIM) from 2015 to 2018. I greatly enjoy online-collaboration with mathematicians and computer scientists on various problems of interest. Most problems involve significant amount of coding for generation and analysis of data. Most math results are either completely generated by computers or at least assisted by a computer. Currently, I am extending my research interests to Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Quantum Computing (QC).
My research interests are:
Mathematics and Computer Science:
Automated Theorem Proving (using SAT/DPLL-like techniques and with Proof-assistants) • Integer & Zero-Sum Ramsey Theory • Discrete Geometry • Algebra • Graphs • Combinatorial Search Algorithms
Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning:
Automated Theorem Proving (AI/ML-Assited) • Deep Natural Language Processing (NLP): theory and applications
Quantum Computing:
Quantum Information Theory • Quantum Cryptography • Algorithms for Deep Learning
